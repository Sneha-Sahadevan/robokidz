Meet RoboKidz

Our Mission & Journey

Fostering the next generation of engineers, digital creators, and critical thinkers through structured STEM innovation programs.

How RoboKidz Started

RoboKidz was founded in 2021 by a team of robotics researchers and educators who noticed a critical gap: school science curricula were dry, static, and lacked practical implementation tools. They wanted to make engineering feel as intuitive and engaging as building with toy blocks.

We designed our own children-safe prototyping kits, combined visual logic blocks with physical sensory boards, and created an active space where failure is celebrated as a key step of the debugging journey. Today, we empower thousands of students to dream big, build smart, and code creatively.

Core Beliefs

Our Guiding Values

These four foundational pillars guide how we write curriculum, design labs, and mentor young engineers.

Curiosity First

We encourage kids to ask "Why?" and "How does it work?" rather than just memorizing theoretical definitions.

Hands-On Doing

True understanding comes from building. Children construct mechanical chassis and write real lines of code from Day 1.

Safe Exploration

We create supportive classrooms where students learn resilience by troubleshooting code and components.

Inclusive Play

We build our modules to welcome girls, boys, coders, builders, and artists alike. STEM is for every creative kid.

Our Journey

How We've Grown

Trace the evolutionary path of RoboKidz from a local lab workshop to a nationwide school STEM provider.

2022

The First Lab Launch

We opened our doors in Bangalore with a single small lab room, custom LEGO-style chassis plates, and 24 enthusiastic pioneer students.

2023

School Integrations

Partnered with 15 regional private academies to establish weekly physical computing labs, replacing standard textbook computer lectures.

2024

National Robotics Award

RoboKidz students won the National STEM Hackathon Junior Division, designing an automatic solar water conservation pump mesh.

2025

AI & VR Curriculum

Created block-based computer vision classes, enabling 10-year-olds to safely train image recognition models and command servos.
